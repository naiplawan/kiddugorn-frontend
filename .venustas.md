# kiddugorn — Design Context

One source of truth for design decisions on this project. Update when decisions change; do not duplicate into component comments.

## Product
**คิดดูก่อน (Kiddugorn)** — a no-login group scheduling/voting tool. You create a poll of date options, share a link, friends vote yes/maybe/no, the most-popular date wins. Thai-first.

## Audience
**Casual Thai friend groups.** Phone-first, low-stakes decisions (dinner, trip, hangout). Product is discovered via a LINE message from a friend — the landing page is often the first touch, but frequently users jump straight to `/create` without ever seeing it.

Not the audience: enterprise teams, HR, formal work scheduling. Secondary audience (small teams) is welcome but must NOT drive tone.

## Brand Tone
**Warm, playful, Thai-expressive.** Hand-made rather than factory-polished. Think Thai food-stall signage, market color, a friend who texts in voice notes — not a SaaS pitch deck.

Three words: **warm, fast, honest.**

## The Hook
**"No login, 10 seconds to share."** Every design decision must serve this promise. If a screen, section, animation, or word slows that down without adding proportional value, cut it. The landing page should prove the claim, not describe it.

## What to Avoid
- SaaS landing template structure (hero → features → stats → testimonials → FAQ → CTA). It reads as AI-generated by default.
- Fake social proof. No fabricated user counts, no written-by-us testimonials, no cycling "live activity" tickers. If we don't have real numbers, we don't show them.
- Enterprise palette (indigo + amber corporate). Purple-to-pink gradients are banned outright.
- English marketing taglines under Thai branding ("Scheduling Made Easy"). Thai-first means Thai-only in the chrome.
- Ambient motion. Floating emoji, pulsing orbs, spinning dashed rings, shimmer sweeps on static cards — all tells of "trying too hard," opposite of confident/fast.
- Decoration stacks — dotted pattern + grid + orbs + radial gradient layered in one viewport.

## Palette — Thai-Market Warmth
Light mode only (for now — landing is a daytime-on-LINE-on-phone surface). Dark mode tokens stay defined but are not a design priority.

- **Background**: jasmine cream — very light warm off-white, tinted toward ochre
- **Foreground**: warm charcoal — deep warm brown-black, never pure black
- **Primary**: tumeric / mango ochre — saturated warm yellow-orange, the hero color
- **Secondary**: warm coral — sits between ochre and chili, used sparingly
- **Accent (chili)**: deep warm red — reserved for the primary CTA and nothing else
- **Success**: warm-leaning forest green (not neon emerald)
- **Neutrals**: all tinted toward the ochre hue (H ~35-42). No cool greys.

Rule: 60% neutral surface / 30% secondary text & borders / 10% accent. Chili-red only on the main CTA. Never use gradients for text. Never mix purple/pink/violet.

## Typography
- **Body**: Kanit (already loaded) — fine for body and UI
- **Display**: must pair with a distinctive Thai display face for headlines. Kanit-for-everything is the Thai equivalent of "Inter for everything" and reads as default. Audition: Chonburi, Bai Jamjuree, Mitr, IBM Plex Sans Thai Looped.
- Type scale: 5 steps, ≥1.25 ratio.

## Motion
One intentional motion moment per surface, not many ambient ones. Use exponential easing (ease-out-quart/quint/expo), never bounce/elastic. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Surfaces & Priority
1. **Landing `/` (public)** — proves the speed promise. Currently: canonical SaaS slop. Being rebuilt.
2. **Create `/create`** — the actual hot path. Most users skip the landing entirely.
3. **Vote `/v/[eventId]`** — the page friends receive. The magic surface. Must feel honest and quick.
4. **Admin `/a/[eventId]`** — lower priority.
